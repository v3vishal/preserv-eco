  • Increased water holding capacity in soils: This builds drought resilience.
  • Lower soil density: As you increase carbon, density decreases because organic compounds are less dense than the soil minerals. When soil is less dense, the roots can travel through the soil and scavenge for nutrients and water more easily.
  • Increased water infiltration: Organic carbon helps to form soil aggregates, where organic molecules produced by microorganisms bind mineral particles together.
  • Increased nutrient availability: When microbes feed on soil organic carbon (in order to get energy), they release nitrogen and phosphorus that were tied to that carbon, thereby providing more nutrients for the plant.
  • Improved trafficability: Meaning that the soil structure is improved and allows farming equipment to traverse the field more days in a given year.

    To increase the SOC and SOM in your soil, you can:
  • Reduce tillage to minimize soil carbon losses and to slow organic matter decomposition processes.
  • Apply fertilizers on cover crops and legumes to produce more biomass.
  • Apply manure, plant material, or other carbon-rich waste.
  • Graze, rather than harvest forage.

    • By improving the SOC and SOM in your soil, you can enhance the health and productivity of your plants. However, the exact number of plants that can benefit will depend on various factors such as the type of plants, their spacing, the specific soil conditions, and the local climate.
